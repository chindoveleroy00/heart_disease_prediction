About This Project

HEART DISEASE PREDICTION: A Machine Learning Approach for Early Detection of Heart Disease

Background

Heart disease remains a significant health challenge in Zimbabwe. With increasing lifestyle-related risk factors, such as hypertension and diabetes, the urgency for effective predictive tools is paramount. Parirenyatwa Group of Hospitals, as a major healthcare provider, can leverage patient data to enhance heart disease management. Implementing a predictive model will not only aid in early detection but also optimize resource allocation and improve overall healthcare delivery in the region.

Problem Statement

Heart disease is a leading cause of mortality in Zimbabwe, affecting thousands annually. The lack of timely and accurate prediction tools hampers early diagnosis and treatment. This project aims to address this gap by utilizing data-driven insights to predict heart disease risk among patients at Parirenyatwa Group of Hospitals.

Aim

To develop and implement a machine learning-based predictive analytics model to assess disease risk and improve early detection of chronic conditions at healthcare institutions, with a specific focus on Parirenyatwa Group of Hospitals.

Objectives

  1. Develop a predictive model for heart disease using patient data.
  2. Improve early diagnosis through data analysis.
  3. Enhance patient outcomes by identifying at-risk individuals.
  4. Provide training for hospital staff on using the predictive tool.

Research Gap/Innovation

Existing Gaps:
  • Healthcare systems rely on reactive diagnostic methods, focusing on treatment after symptoms develop rather than predicting risks early.
  • Limited use of predictive analytics and machine learning in Zimbabwe's healthcare sector, despite available patient data.
Innovations:
  • Utilizes advanced machine learning algorithms to predict disease risks based on patient data.
  • Provides actionable insights for early interventions, potentially reducing healthcare costs and improving patient outcomes.
  • Incorporates local data from Parirenyatwa Group of Hospitals to create a model tailored to Zimbabwe's healthcare challenges.

How The Model Works

The heart disease prediction model uses a machine learning algorithm called Random Forest that has been trained on patient data. The model takes into account various factors such as:

  • Patient demographics (age, sex)
  • Clinical measurements (blood pressure, cholesterol, etc.)
  • Medical history (diabetes, hypertension, family history)
  • Lifestyle factors (smoking)
  • Test results (ECG findings, exercise test results)

Based on these inputs, the model calculates the probability of heart disease presence, which can help healthcare providers identify at-risk patients and initiate preventive measures or early treatments.
